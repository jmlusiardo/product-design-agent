# Executive Briefing Template: Impact-First Approval Ask

> **Purpose:** Fill in this template before any meeting where you're seeking budget, headcount, or
> roadmap approval. Complete all 4 layers before building slides. The goal is to open with what
> leadership stands to *lose* — not what your team *did*.

---

## Layer 1 — What's at stake

**Sentence structure:**
```
"We're currently [losing / risking / falling behind on] [specific metric] because [root cause].
That represents [business consequence in $, users, or competitive position]."
```

**Your draft:**
> We're currently _________________ because _________________.
> That represents _________________.

**Examples:**
- "We're losing ~12,000 MAU at the onboarding settings screen. That's our fastest-growing churn point
  and it's accelerating."
- "Our enterprise clients average 3.2 support tickets/month on report generation — double our target.
  That's $340K annually and the #1 reason cited in exit surveys."
- "We're 18 months behind both primary competitors on mobile self-service. Mobile engagement is 41%
  below industry benchmark, and Q4 research shows it's the top reason prospects choose [Competitor]."

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Opens with consequence, not process or methodology
- [ ] Metric is specific and quantified
- [ ] Business consequence stated in $ / users / competitive terms
- [ ] No "we conducted X interviews" in the opening

---

## Layer 2 — What changes if you act

**Sentence structure:**
```
"We have a [validated / tested / proven] approach that [specific improvement] in [timeframe].
Based on [evidence], we project [metric change]."
```

**Your draft:**
> We have a _________________ approach that _________________ in _________________.
> Based on _________________, we project _________________.

**Examples:**
- "We've validated a simplified flow that reduced task abandonment by 58% in testing. If those results
  hold at scale, we recover ~7,000 of those 12,000 monthly users."
- "We've tested a guided report builder that cut average task time from 14 min to 4. That eliminates
  ~60% of those support tickets — roughly $200K in annual savings."

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Recommendation framed as outcome ("reduces churn"), not deliverable ("redesigns the dashboard")
- [ ] Evidence base named (testing, benchmarks, prior data)
- [ ] Projected metric change connects back to Layer 1 numbers

---

## Layer 3 — What it costs and why it's worth it

**Sentence structure:**
```
"This requires [specific resource commitment]. It means [what gets deprioritized or delayed].
It's worth it because [Layer 1 consequence > cost]."
```

**Your draft:**
> This requires _________________.
> It means _________________.
> It's worth it because _________________.

**Note:** If you skip this layer, leadership fills the tradeoff blank with their worst assumption.
Naming it yourself signals that you've weighed the same variables they're weighing.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Resource ask is specific (engineering weeks, headcount, budget range)
- [ ] Tradeoff named explicitly — don't omit or soften
- [ ] ROI argument closes the loop to Layer 1 numbers

---

## Layer 4 — The ask and the fallback

**Sentence structure:**
```
"I'm asking for [specific resource/approval] by [date].
If the full scope isn't possible, my fallback is [smaller version / pilot / phased approach]."
```

**Your draft:**
> I'm asking for _________________ by _________________.
> If the full scope isn't possible, my fallback is _________________.

**Why the fallback matters:** It removes "no" from the table. The only available answers become
"yes" or "yes, but smaller" — both are progress.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Ask is specific — no vague "support" or "alignment"
- [ ] Timeline anchored to a date, sprint, or quarter
- [ ] Fallback option is real and achievable (not a token gesture)
- [ ] "No" is not an available answer

---

## Slide Structure (5 slides max)

| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | The 4-layer statement — this is the only slide that fully matters |
| 2 | Competitive landscape + market evidence supporting Layer 1 |
| 3 | The tradeoff — what gets deprioritized and why it's worth it |
| 4 | Risk of inaction — projected cost if nothing changes |
| 5 | The ask + fallback |

---

## Pre-flight Checklist

Before the meeting:
- [ ] All 4 layers drafted and reviewed
- [ ] Layer 1 metric verified (not estimated)
- [ ] Slide 1 contains the complete 4-layer argument
- [ ] Fallback is concrete and pre-approved by your team
- [ ] Pre-aligned with at least one attending stakeholder (nemawashi)
